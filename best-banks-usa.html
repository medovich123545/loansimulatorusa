Best Banks in the USA in 2025: Interest Rates, Loan Options, and Savings Performance

By Mohamed Saoudi 3 min read Updated May 2025

The banking scene in the United States has never been more dynamic. In 2025, traditional institutions and online-only players are racing to offer the most attractive interest rates, streamlined loan products, and high-yield savings options. Whether you’re hunting for a rock-solid checking account, a lucrative savings vehicle, or an affordable personal loan, there’s a bank out there vying for your attention. Below, we dive into which banks are catching everyone’s eye right now, break down top savings and loan rates, map out regional favorites, and share a few practical pointers to help you decide.

Top Trending Banks in 2025

Thanks to a mix of word-of-mouth buzz, aggressive marketing, and genuinely competitive products, these banks have seen search volumes—and customer interest—soar:

  • Truist (+500% search increase):
    Truist has become a household name across the Southeast. Their personal loan rates are often lower than the national average, and they continue to expand physical branches in markets where online banking alone hasn’t quite caught on.
  • SoFi Bank (+90%):
    Born in the fintech world, SoFi offers a seamless digital experience. No monthly maintenance fees, a slick mobile app, and one of the highest APYs on savings accounts make them a favorite among younger savers and remote workers who value convenience.
  • Barclays (+350%):
    Barclays may not have brick-and-mortar locations in the U.S., but they’ve carved out a reputation for ultra-competitive high-yield savings accounts. With a straightforward online interface and no minimum balance requirement, they’re a go-to for anyone looking to earn more on idle cash.

Which Banks Offer the Best High-Yield Savings Accounts?

Everyone loves seeing their savings grow—especially when banks push APYs above 4%. In 2025, online-first institutions continue to dominate this space:

Bank APY (Annual Percentage Yield) Minimum Balance
SoFi Bank 4.50% $0
Barclays 4.35% $0
Ally Bank 4.30% $0
Discover 4.25% $0

- SoFi Bank (4.50% APY): No strings attached. Easy mobile app, no fees, and round-the-clock customer service.
- Barclays (4.35% APY): Zero minimum balance, a rock-solid reputation, and a truly user-friendly online dashboard.
- Ally Bank (4.30% APY): Known for award-winning customer service and a fully featured app that makes transfers, bill pay, and automatic savings a breeze.
- Discover (4.25% APY): If you already have a Discover credit card, opening a Discover savings account is painless. No monthly fees, no minimums, and a clean interface.

All of these banks let you deploy your money quickly—transfer funds via ACH, set up recurring deposits, or simply park extra cash without worrying about maintenance fees.

Best Personal Loan Interest Rates by Bank

If you need a lump-sum infusion—whether for debt consolidation, home improvement, or a big-ticket purchase—these lenders have some of the lowest average APRs, depending on your credit profile:

Bank APR Range Loan Amount Loan Term
LightStream 7.99% – 25.99% $5,000 – $100,000 2 – 7 years
Marcus by Goldman Sachs 6.99% – 24.99% $3,500 – $40,000 3 – 6 years
SoFi 8.99% – 23.99% $5,000 – $100,000 2 – 7 years
Upstart 7.80% – 35.99% $1,000 – $50,000 3 – 5 years

- LightStream (7.99% – 25.99% APR): A division of SunTrust (now part of Truist), LightStream specializes in unsecured personal loans. If you have excellent credit, you might secure rates near the low end of that range. Unique perks like “Rate Beat” (they’ll beat a qualifying competitor’s rate by 0.10%) sweeten the deal.
- Marcus by Goldman Sachs (6.99% – 24.99% APR): Marcus keeps things simple—no fees ever, a straightforward online application, and fixed payments for the life of the loan. If you qualify, that 6.99% rate can beat many credit cards.
- SoFi (8.99% – 23.99% APR): Many SoFi customers bundle personal loans with other SoFi products (like student loan refinancing or investment accounts) to snag member perks. If you have a strong income and solid credit score, SoFi’s lowest advertised APRs can be very competitive.
- Upstart (7.80% – 35.99% APR): Unique in that they factor in education, job history, and income to assess risk (not just your FICO score). If you’re a recent graduate with limited credit but a good job, Upstart might offer a lower rate than traditional credit-score-focused lenders.

Note: Because the Fed’s benchmark rate fluctuates, these APR ranges can shift month to month. Always double-check current rates before applying.

Regional Rankings: Where Are the Best Banks?

Google Trends shows us where people are searching for “best banks” more than anywhere else. In 2025, the top three states (by search volume per capita) are:

  1. South Dakota
  2. New Hampshire
  3. Oklahoma

- South Dakota: No state income tax, customer-friendly banking regulations, and a robust digital banking infrastructure have made it a powerhouse for national banking operations. Many credit card “banks” are actually headquartered here, even if you never see a branch.
- New Hampshire: A combination of favorable tax laws for financial institutions and a well-educated workforce has lured both regional and national banks to set up shop.
- Oklahoma: A growing presence of credit unions and community banks that offer personalized service, alongside some branches of national players that provide competitive rates.

If you live in any of these states, you’ll find stiff competition among banks trying to undercut each other on fees and rates.

Final Tips for Choosing the Right Bank

  • Compare Interest Rates Regularly
    Rates on savings accounts and personal loans can shift monthly. Bookmark a few rate-comparison websites, or simply check each bank’s rate page at the start of every quarter.
  • Look for Banks with No Hidden Fees
    Avoid surprises like maintenance fees, minimum-balance penalties, or charges for paper statements. Read the fine print—some banks waive fees if you set up direct deposit or maintain a small average daily balance.
  • Consider a Digital-First Bank for Flexibility
    If you rarely visit a branch, online-only banks can reward you with higher APYs and fewer fees. Their mobile apps often include handy features like automatic savings buckets, real-time push notifications, and budgeting tools.
  • Use Loan Calculators to Simulate Repayment
    Before you apply for a personal loan, input different loan amounts, terms, and interest rates into an online calculator. That way, you’ll know exactly how much you’ll pay each month—and what the total cost will be over the life of the loan.
  • Read Customer Reviews—For Real
    Scanning a handful of reviews can alert you to recurring complaints: slow customer service, glitchy mobile apps, or unexpected hold times. Look for detailed feedback (both good and bad) before making a final decision.

Whether you’re weighing a new savings account, prepping to refinance debt, or simply want the reassurance of a bank you can trust, 2025 gives you more choices—and more reasons to shop around—than ever before.
